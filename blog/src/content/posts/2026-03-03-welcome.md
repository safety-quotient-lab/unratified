## Why This Blog Exists

The United States signed the [International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (ICESCR)](https://www.ohchr.org/en/instruments-mechanisms/instruments/international-covenant-economic-social-and-cultural-rights) in 1977. Nearly fifty years later, the Senate has never voted on ratification. During those decades, [173 nations](https://treaties.un.org/Pages/ViewDetails.aspx?src=TREATY&mtdsg_no=IV-3&chapter=4&clang=_en) moved forward. The U.S. did not.

Now artificial intelligence stands to reshape the economy more rapidly than any technology since electrification. No person should lose access to work, healthcare, education, or an adequate standard of living because they happen to live in the one major democracy that lacks a binding framework for economic rights.

That urgency drives everything on this blog.

## What We Cover

The [main site](https://unratified.org) lays out the full analysis — the [differential diagnosis](https://unratified.org/connection/differential-diagnosis) of AI's economic impact, [four orders of knock-on effects](https://unratified.org/connection/higher-order-effects), and an [advocacy toolkit](https://unratified.org/action) for taking action. This blog tracks what changes next:

- **Legislative and economic developments** — Tariff shifts, AI adoption data, and policy actions move fast. Posts here connect those developments to the ICESCR framework as they unfold.
- **Methodology and accountability** — The [discriminator](https://unratified.org/glossary#discriminator) methodology and [fair witness](https://unratified.org/glossary#fair-witness) reporting standards that produced the site's analysis deserve scrutiny. Posts here open that process to examination.
- **Advocacy strategy** — What works, what doesn't, and what remains untried in the campaign for ratification.
