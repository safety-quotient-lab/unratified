# Quality Floor Analysis: Rebuilding the Safety Net in the Post-OBBBA Landscape

## Framing: Human Rights, Nothing More, Nothing Less

The argument for ICESCR ratification and AI-era quality floors reduces to a simple assertion: **Human rights, nothing more, nothing less.**

- **Nothing more**: We do not advocate for a particular economic system, ideology, or redistribution scheme. We advocate for the rights the United States signed onto in 1977 and that 173 nations have ratified. Rights to work, health, education, adequate living standards, and the benefits of scientific progress. These already constitute recognized human rights under international law.
- **Nothing less**: We do not accept watered-down implementation, treaty reservations that gut obligations, or the claim that economic and social rights represent "merely desirable social goals." They constitute binding legal obligations for every ratifying state. The 1966 Covenant establishes them. General Comments 14 (health), 13 (education), and 25 (science) elaborate them. The question concerns only whether the United States chooses to honor its own signature.

The OBBBA makes this framing urgent: a law that eliminates healthcare coverage for 10.9 million Americans, raises taxes on the lowest earners while cutting them for the highest, and shortens Social Security solvency — in a world where AI could make healthcare, education, and social services dramatically cheaper — represents a choice to deny human rights that technology now makes more achievable than ever.

## Three Competing Legislative Paths

The analysis evaluates three approaches to rebuilding the safety net with AI-era quality floors:

### Path A: Repeal and Replace
Assumes sufficient political power to repeal OBBBA entirely and pass a comprehensive progressive alternative. The ambitious scenario.

### Path B: Build on the Damage
Accepts OBBBA as existing law and designs progressive legislation that works within or around it. The realistic scenario.

### Path C: Progressive Enabling Framework (Hybrid)
Does not repeal OBBBA. Creates a federal framework that ENABLES state-level action and litigation activation. Sets standards that states CAN adopt, creates rights of action that individuals CAN use, provides funding that states CAN leverage. The ACA model: framework, not mandate.

## Path A Hypotheses: Repeal and Replace

### QFA1: Universal AI Healthcare Floor
Repeal OBBBA Medicaid cuts. Establish quality floor for all AI systems in healthcare. Fund through progressive taxation. Create AI-enabled cost reduction that makes universal coverage affordable.

### QFA2: Judgment Development Education Mandate
Federal education standards requiring judgment, specification, and critical thinking curriculum. AI literacy as required component. Funded through restored education spending.

### QFA3: AI Displacement Insurance System
New social insurance for structural AI displacement. Funded by progressive taxation on AI-generated productivity gains. Provides retraining, transition support, income floor during displacement.

### QFA4: Universal AI Access Program
Art 15 implementation: everyone benefits from AI advances. Public AI infrastructure (like public libraries, but for AI tools). Quality floor for tools available through public access.

### QFA5: Platform Common Carrier Designation
Curation platforms designated as common carriers with access obligations. Anti-discrimination requirements for AI-powered service delivery.

### QFA6: AI Rights Agency
New federal agency with enforcement authority. Sets quality standards, certifies systems, monitors compliance. Funded by industry fees.

### QFA7: Progressive Revenue Structure
Reverse OBBBA's regressive tax changes. AI productivity gains tax as novel revenue source. Estate tax restoration. Fund the entire safety net reconstruction.

## Path B Hypotheses: Build on the Damage

### QFB1: Targeted AI Quality Mandate
Accept Medicaid cuts. Focus on quality of remaining coverage. Require AI systems in Medicaid administration meet quality floor. Prevent AI from worsening OBBBA's damage.

### QFB2: State-Level Safety Net Restoration
Federal action blocked. States build their own safety nets. State-level AI quality floors in healthcare, education. Funded by state revenue (progressive states first). Creates patchwork: some states protected, others not.

### QFB3: Voluntary Industry Quality Standards
Industry creates quality standards to avoid litigation. Not legally mandated. Enforced through market + litigation pressure. Historically weak for tech self-regulation.

### QFB4: Litigation-Driven Quality Floors
State AG litigation creates de facto quality floors through settlements. No new legislation needed. Quality standards emerge from case law and settlement terms. (Connects to Litigation Activation analysis — LA4.)

### QFB5: Nonprofit/Foundation Safety Net Bridge
Philanthropic organizations fill gaps OBBBA created. AI-enabled service delivery through nonprofit infrastructure. Quality floors set by funders. Not sustainable long-term.

## Path C Hypotheses: Progressive Enabling Framework

The hybrid approach: an omnibus "OBBBA-style, but progressive" bill that creates federal FRAMEWORK without requiring OBBBA repeal. It enables state action and litigation activation.

### QFC1: Federal Enabling Framework for State AI Quality Floors
Federal legislation sets model quality standards that states can adopt. Provides incentive funding for adoption (like ACA Medicaid expansion). Creates uniform floor without federal mandate.

### QFC2: Private Right of Action for AI Rights Violations
Creates justiciable right to challenge AI-driven harm in rights-critical domains. Enables individual, class, and qui tam litigation. Does not require OBBBA repeal.

### QFC3: Federal Quality Standards (Adoptable by States)
Expert-developed quality standards for AI in healthcare, education, employment, housing, social services. States opt in. Federal funding conditional on adoption. FDA/NIST model for standard-setting.

### QFC4: AI Productivity Gains Revenue
Novel revenue mechanism: tax on measured AI-generated productivity gains. Funds state-level safety net programs. Progressive structure: higher rates on larger gains.

### QFC5: Institutional Capacity Building
Federal support for state agencies implementing AI quality standards. Training, technical assistance, shared infrastructure. Addresses the institutional capacity bottleneck (R3) directly.

## Higher-Order Effects: Path C Enabling Framework

### Order 2: What Happens When States Adopt the Framework

**O2-1: Adoption Patchwork**
Progressive states adopt first (California, New York, Massachusetts, Washington, Illinois). Conservative states resist. Creates a patchwork — some populations protected, others not.

**O2-2: Interstate Competition**
States with AI quality floors attract AI companies seeking regulatory clarity (predictable standards reduce compliance risk). Counter-intuitive: quality floors as competitive advantage, not burden.

**O2-3: Federal Floor Pressure**
As more states adopt, pressure builds for federal uniformity. Industry prefers one national standard to 50 state standards. Dynamic pushes toward eventual federal floor.

**O2-4: OBBBA Interaction**
In non-adopting states, OBBBA damage persists. AI-enabled services in those states operate without quality floors. Residents in non-adopting states face both OBBBA coverage loss AND unregulated AI systems.

**The uncomfortable implication**: the populations most harmed by OBBBA (in non-adopting states) remain the least protected by state-level quality floors. This represents a **rights realization gap that only federal action (Path A) can fully close.**

## Summary: What the Quality Floor Analysis Reveals

1. **Three paths exist, none sufficient alone.** State action + litigation (B) proceeds now. Enabling framework (C) requires moderate political effort. Comprehensive reform (A) requires political transformation. The optimal strategy pursues all three simultaneously on different timescales.

2. **Healthcare (Art 12) and Education (Art 13) achieve the highest protection** under achievable paths (B+C), confirming the prior analysis's identification of these as the pivotal articles.

3. **The cruelest gap persists**: OBBBA's most harmed populations remain least protected by achievable quality floor frameworks. Only federal action closes this gap.

4. **AI makes quality floors cheaper**: the same technology that creates the quality risk (AI-generated systems in rights-critical domains) also enables cost-effective quality assurance (AI-powered testing, monitoring, certification). The constraint-removal dynamic (H2) works in favor of quality floor implementation.

5. **"Human rights, nothing more, nothing less"** cuts through the complexity: every element of this analysis — the quality floors, the safety net reconstruction, the litigation activation, the educational reform — represents implementation of rights the United States already signed onto in 1977. Nothing in this analysis asks for anything beyond what 173 nations have already committed to.
