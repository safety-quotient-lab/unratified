# Unratified — Pedagogical Website for ICESCR Advocacy

## Project Vision

**Unratified** advocates for U.S. ratification of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights (ICESCR) through a contemporary lens: the economic impact of AI. The site demonstrates that AI-driven economic transformation makes ICESCR ratification more urgent than at any point since the Covenant's adoption in 1966.

The project name refers to the United States' status as one of only a handful of nations that signed but never ratified the ICESCR (signed 1977, Senate consent never sought).

### Tagline

> "One of Anthropic Claude's takes on the impact of AI on the economy — differential diagnosis, integral chain analysis, and what it means for your rights"

NOTE: The framing acknowledges this analysis as one perspective generated by Claude (Anthropic's AI), grounding the meta-layer — an AI analyzing AI's own economic impact — as part of the site's intellectual honesty. Candidate variations:
- "An AI's differential diagnosis of AI's economic impact — and the treaty that could change who benefits"
- "Claude's take: differential diagnosis and chain analysis of AI's knock-on effects on the economy"
- "What happens when an AI analyzes AI's impact on your economic rights?"

## Core Thesis

AI functions as narrow superintelligence for software labor, removing constraints that previously blocked economic activity. This triggers a Jevons-style demand explosion, bounded by new bottlenecks (regulation, energy, trust, human judgment), distributed unevenly across adopters and non-adopters. The resulting economic bifurcation — between those who benefit from AI and those who do not — maps directly onto the rights the ICESCR protects: work, adequate living standards, health, education, and the right to benefit from scientific progress (Article 15).

Without a binding legal framework for economic, social, and cultural rights, the U.S. has no structural mechanism to ensure AI's benefits reach everyone.

The recursive higher-order analysis reveals that bottlenecks migrate from technical capability toward human capacities — judgment, specification, curation, and ultimately values/meaning. Article 13 (Education) emerges as the most pivotal ICESCR provision across all analytical orders, because judgment capability (the new scarce resource) develops through practice and experience that AI cannot substitute.

### Advocacy Framing: "Human Rights; Nothing More, Nothing Less."

Every element of this analysis — quality floors, safety net reconstruction, litigation activation, educational reform — represents implementation of rights the United States already signed onto in 1977. Nothing here asks for anything beyond what 173 nations have already committed to.

### Post-OBBBA Context

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (P.L. 119-21, July 2025) slashed $990B from Medicaid, eliminated coverage for 10.9M Americans, and structured tax changes that decrease income for the lowest 10% while increasing it for the highest 10%. The analysis identifies three paths for rebuilding the safety net:
- **Path B (NOW)**: State action + litigation — proceeds without federal legislation
- **Path C (NEXT)**: Progressive enabling framework — federal standards states can adopt
- **Path A (EVENTUALLY)**: Comprehensive reform — requires political transformation

The dominant enforcement mechanism across all paths: **State AG litigation** (scoring 20/25), following the tobacco Master Settlement pattern.

## Audience Architecture (Three-Tier Pipeline + Delivery Channel)

Six personas across three tiers. Entry personas discover content; the conversion persona acts on it; the target persona receives that action.

### Tier 1 — Entry (How people find the site)

1. **Hackers** (observatory.unratified.org — "Human Rights Observatory, Hacker News Edition")
   - Software developers, engineers, AI practitioners, technical founders
   - They evaluate and amplify the analysis (distribution engine)
   - Entry: HN front page → observatory analysis → discover ICESCR → advocacy

2. **Teachers** (unratified.org/educators)
   - High school civics/social studies, university human rights/economics professors
   - They shape the judgment pipeline (the Article 13 pivot in action)
   - Entry: colleague/search → educator hub → lesson plans → classroom use

3. **Scientists / Academics** (observatory.unratified.org/methodology)
   - Economics, international law, STS, AI impact researchers
   - They validate and extend the methodology (credibility engine)
   - Entry: citation/conference → methodology → evaluate → cite/extend

### Tier 2 — Conversion (The moment understanding becomes action)

5. **Voters** (unratified.org/action)
   - Not a demographic — a functional role. Anyone from any entry persona who decides to act.
   - "I understand — now what do I DO?"
   - Conversion bridge: `/action/why-act` (built)
   - The /action section serves this persona; persona documented (D015)

### Tier 3 — Target (Who receives the advocacy)

6. **Politicians / Legislative Staff** (unratified.org/action/policy-brief)
   - The site addresses politicians indirectly, through voter pressure
   - Functional persona: the legislative aide who evaluates and briefs the senator
   - Policy brief: `/action/policy-brief` (built) with floor-speech talking points
   - D015: documented as target persona

### Delivery Channel

4. **Commercial Educational Publishers** *(license cleared, not yet engaged)*
   - Textbook publishers, EdTech platforms, curriculum distributors
   - CC BY-SA 4.0 permits commercial use (D014)

See plan.md.marketing for complete user journeys, UX constraints, and the full pipeline diagram.

## Content Strategy

### Pedagogical Approach
- **Progressive disclosure**: surface-level accessible, depth available on demand
- **Fair witness framing**: observe without interpretation, distinguish observation from inference
- **E-prime communication**: avoid forms of "to be" in user-facing copy
- **Multiple entry points**: visitors arrive from AI/tech interest, human rights interest, or education interest — each path leads to the core thesis
- **Evidence-first**: every claim linked to source, every inference marked as inference

### Tone
- Professional, supportive, intellectually honest
- Inspired by the clarity of Asimov's science writing and Heinlein's directness
- Accessible to people with cognitive difficulties (WCAG-aligned language complexity)
- No preaching — present evidence and let readers draw conclusions
